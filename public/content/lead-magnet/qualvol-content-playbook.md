# The QualVol Content Playbook

## Quality + Volume: The Modern Social Media Operating System

A playbook for owners and marketing leaders who refuse to choose between
quality and volume. Inside: the swarm model, scoring rubrics, distribution
matrices, and the exact workflow we use to ship hundreds of on-brand posts
per week across every platform.

---

## Part 1 — Why "Pick One" Is Dead

Every agency tells you to pick. Quality posts that sit in a vacuum, or
volume content that bores your audience to death. Both fail. The platforms
reward consistent, varied, on-brand publishing — and that requires both.

### The QualVol Equation

`Reach = (Unique Hooks × Platforms × Frequency) × Brand Fit Score`

Pull any lever to zero and reach collapses. The whole point of the
playbook is to keep every lever above its threshold without burning out
your team.

---

## Part 2 — The Swarm Model

A swarm is a coordinated set of accounts that publish around a single
brand:

1. **Brand account** — authoritative, polished, the storefront.
2. **Personal brand** — founder/operator face, behind-the-scenes voice.
3. **Niche pages** — topical accounts that capture intent (e.g., a
   roofing brand running a "Storm Damage Tips" page).

Each account targets a different layer of awareness. Together they
multiply share-of-voice without diluting the brand.

---

## Part 3 — The Brand Fit Score

Every piece of content is scored 0–100 before it ships:

- **Voice match** (0–25)
- **Visual identity** (0–25)
- **Audience relevance** (0–25)
- **Hook strength** (0–25)

Anything under 70 gets reworked. Anything 70–85 ships to niche pages.
85+ ships to the brand account.

## Part 5 — The Distribution Matrix

A single hook → 7 platforms → 21 variants:

- **Instagram**: Reel, Carousel, Story
- **TikTok**: Hook + payoff + CTA
- **YouTube Shorts**: Vertical, captions burnt-in
- **Facebook**: Reel + native post
- **LinkedIn**: Text + carousel
- **Threads**: 280-char hook
- **X**: Quote + thread

## Part 7 — KPIs That Actually Matter

- **Posts shipped per week** (volume floor)
- **Brand fit score average** (quality floor)
- **Profile visits** (intent leading indicator)
- **Saves + shares** (algorithmic fuel)
- **Booked calls** (revenue truth)

Vanity metrics (followers, likes) are reported but never optimized for.
