## What's Actually Sitting in Your Database?

Most businesses don't think of their CRM as a "database" — it's just where customer info lives. But every contact represents a past moment of intent:

| Segment | What it is | Why it's valuable |
|---|---|---|
| Unsold estimates | Got a quote, never booked | The need likely still exists |
| Missed calls | Called but nobody answered | Highest-intent segment |
| Old paid leads | Google/Meta/HomeAdvisor leads from prior months | You already paid for them |
| Past customers | Prior service customers | Trust already exists |
| Lapsed maintenance members | Weren't on a plan that expired | Recurring revenue waiting to happen |
| No-show appointments | Booked but didn't show | Easier than cold outreach |
| Warranty expiring soon | Service warranty approaching end | Natural renewal trigger |
| Seasonal lists | HVAC tune-up, roof inspection, pool opening | Timing creates urgency |

The average home service company has **3,000–15,000 contacts** across these segments. At $0.75–$2.50 per contact for AI-powered reactivation, the total campaign cost is **$2,250–$37,500** — a fraction of what you'd spend acquiring the same number of leads through ads.

## The Timing Windows That Maximize Response

Timing matters more than copy. The same message sent at different times produces wildly different results:

| Timing window | Best segment | Response rate | Why it works |
|---|---|---:|---|
| 0–7 days | Missed calls, unbooked forms | 12–28% | Intent is immediate, need is active |
| 7–30 days | No-decision quotes | 6–18% | Homeowner is still deciding |
| 60–180 days | Delayed replacement decisions | 3–10% | Costs changed, availability opened |
| Pre-season | HVAC, pools, landscaping | 5–15% | "Get ahead before the rush" |
| Storm/weather event | Roofing, restoration | 8–20% | Immediate need, emotional trigger |
| Annual anniversary | Past customers | 5–12% | Natural check-in, trust exists |

AI-powered reactivation uses timing triggers instead of blasting the full list at once — which is why it outperforms manual campaigns by 2–3x.

## How AI Makes Reactivation Actually Doable

Manual database reactivation requires someone to pull lists, write scripts, make calls, log responses, follow up, and book appointments. At scale, that's a full-time job.

AI reactivation automates the entire workflow:

1. **Import contacts** from your CRM, billing system, or spreadsheet

5. **Books appointments** directly on your calendar for hot leads
6. **Follows up** automatically — 2–3 touchpoints over 7–14 days
7. **Reports results** — response rate, booked jobs, revenue generated

## FAQ

**Q: How old can a contact be and still be worth reactivating?**
A: Contacts up to 24 months old still produce measurable response rates. After 24 months, the list quality drops significantly — but even 3-year-old lists can produce 1–3% positive responses if the business relationship was strong.

**Q: Won't people be annoyed by getting called about an old estimate?**
A: A well-crafted reactivation message acknowledges the time gap and offers value (price lock, availability, seasonal timing). Response data shows 3–12% positive replies — most people are grateful someone followed up. Annoyance comes from spammy messaging, not from reaching out.

**Q: Do I need a clean CRM to do this?**
A: No. AI reactivation platforms can work with exported CSVs, phone records, billing exports, or even handwritten customer lists. The data doesn't need to be perfect — it needs to be reachable (valid phone numbers).

**Q: How is this different from an email newsletter?**
A: Newsletters are one-to-many, generic, and have 0.5–2% click rates. Database reactivation is one-to-one, personalized, and has 3–12% positive response rates. Different channel, different intent, different results.
