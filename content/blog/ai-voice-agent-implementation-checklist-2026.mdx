You've decided to deploy an AI voice agent. Now what? Most businesses underestimate the preparation needed and overestimate the time it takes. The reality: a well-prepared business can go live in 7–14 days. An unprepared one takes 4–8 weeks.

This checklist tells you exactly what to have ready before you start, what happens during implementation, and what to expect after go-live — so you can hit day-one benchmarks from the start.

## Key Takeaways

- **Go-live timeline: 7–14 days** for done-for-you platforms, 2–8 weeks for DIY
- **Preparation is the differentiator** — businesses that prepare call scenarios and integrations before starting go live 2x faster
- **Top 10 call scenarios** cover 80–90% of inbound calls — document these first
- **CRM/calendar integration** is required for booking — have your calendar tool ready
- **Phone number strategy** — forward existing number, port, or get new local number
- **Test with real calls** before going live — run 20–50 test calls to catch issues
- **Post-launch tuning** — monitor for 2 weeks, adjust prompts based on actual call data

## Pre-Implementation Checklist (Before Day 1)

### Business Information

- [ ] Business name and location(s)
- [ ] Service area (zip codes or radius)
- [ ] Hours of operation (including holidays)
- [ ] Emergency routing rules (who handles emergencies, what counts as emergency)
- [ ] Contact information for escalation (owner, manager, on-call)

### Call Scenarios (Top 10)

Document your top 10 call scenarios with scripts:

| # | Scenario | Key information to collect | Action to take |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | New service inquiry | Service type, address, urgency, preferred time | Book appointment |
| 2 | Emergency call | Location, nature of emergency | Transfer to on-call / dispatch |
| 3 | Existing customer follow-up | Name, service history, current need | Book service appointment |
| 4 | Pricing inquiry | Service type, property details | Provide estimate range, book consultation |
| 5 | Scheduling/rescheduling | Customer name, preferred time | Reschedule appointment |
| 6 | After-hours inquiry | Service type, urgency | Collect info, schedule callback |
| 7 | Warranty/service plan question | Customer name, plan type | Answer or transfer to billing |
| 8 | Complaint/issue | Customer name, issue description | Collect details, escalate to manager |
| 9 | Referral inquiry | Referrer name, new customer info | Book consultation |
| 10 | General question | Topic | Answer or route to appropriate team |

**The more specific your scenarios, the better your AI agent performs.** Write out the exact questions you want asked and the exact actions for each outcome.

### Technical Requirements

- [ ] **Calendar tool** — Google Calendar, Calendly, or similar (for booking)
- [ ] **CRM access** — ServiceTitan, Housecall Pro, Jobber, HubSpot, or similar
- [ ] **Phone strategy** — forward existing number, port, or new local number
- [ ] **Internet connection** — stable broadband for any web-based integrations
- [ ] **Staff access** — who on your team needs admin access to the platform?

### Content to Prepare

- [ ] **Business description** — 2–3 sentences about what you do
- [ ] **Service list** — all services offered with brief descriptions
- [ ] **Pricing information** — ranges or "call for estimate" policy
- [ ] **FAQ answers** — top 10 questions callers ask with your standard answers
- [ ] **Tone guidelines** — professional, friendly, casual? What words to use/avoid?

## Implementation Timeline: Day-by-Day

### Week 1: Setup & Configuration

| Day | Task | Who | Duration |
|---|---|---|---:|
| Day 1 | Kickoff call — review scenarios, routing, integrations | You + platform team | 60 min |
| Day 2 | Phone number setup — forward, port, or new number | Platform team | 1–2 hours |
| Day 3 | CRM/calendar integration — connect your tools | Platform team | 1–2 hours |
| Day 4 | Prompt engineering — configure AI agent with your scenarios | Platform team | 2–4 hours |
| Day 5 | Internal testing — team members make test calls | Your team | 1 hour |
| Day 6–7 | Adjustments based on test results | Platform team | 1–2 hours |

### Week 2: Testing & Go-Live

| Day | Task | Who | Duration |
|---|---|---|---:|
| Day 8 | Soft launch — forward 20% of calls to AI agent | You | Monitor |
| Day 9–10 | Review call recordings — identify issues | You + platform team | 30 min/day |
| Day 11 | Prompt adjustments based on real call data | Platform team | 1 hour |
| Day 12 | Increase to 50% of calls | You | Monitor |
| Day 13 | Final review — confirm booking accuracy | You + platform team | 30 min |
| Day 14 | Full launch — 100% of calls to AI agent | You | Monitor |

## Post-Launch Monitoring (Weeks 3–4)

| Metric | Target | How to measure |
|---|---:|---|
| Answer rate | 95%+ | Calls answered ÷ total calls |
| Average response time | Under 12 seconds | Platform analytics |
| Booking rate | 45%+ | Bookings ÷ total calls |
| Caller satisfaction | 4+ stars | Post-call survey (optional) |
| Escalation rate | Under 10% | Transfers to human ÷ total calls |
| Booking accuracy | 95%+ | Bookings confirmed ÷ total bookings |

### Common Issues & Fixes

| Issue | Likely cause | Fix |
|---|---|---|
| Low booking rate | Prompt not capturing key info | Add questions to prompt |
| High escalation rate | Scenarios not defined | Add missing scenarios |
| Wrong bookings | Calendar integration issue | Re-sync calendar |
| Call quality issues | Telephony/carrier problem | Check phone number config |
| After-hours calls not handled | Hours not configured | Update hours of operation |

## FAQ

**Q: Do I need technical skills to implement an AI voice agent?**
A: With a done-for-you platform, no. You provide your call scenarios, routing rules, and calendar access — they handle the technical setup. With a DIY platform, you'll need developer resources for configuration and integration.

**Q: What if my calls are in multiple languages?**
A: Most AI voice platforms support English and Spanish natively. Some support additional languages. Specify your language requirements during the kickoff call.

**Q: Can I keep my existing phone number?**
A: Yes. You can either forward your existing number to the AI agent (quickest) or port the number to the new platform (takes 1–2 weeks). Forwarding is recommended for the fastest go-live.

**Q: What happens if the AI can't handle a call?**
A: The AI agent transfers the call to a designated human (you, your manager, or an on-call team member). Escalation rules are configured during setup — you define what triggers a transfer.

**Q: How do I know if the AI is booking correctly?**
A: Monitor your calendar for the first 2 weeks. Confirm bookings match what the caller requested. Most platforms provide call recordings and booking logs for review.
