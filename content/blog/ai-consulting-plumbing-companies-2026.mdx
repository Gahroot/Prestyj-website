## Emergency Triaging: Burst Pipes vs. Faucet Repairs

The single biggest operational improvement AI brings to plumbing companies is intelligent emergency triage. Most plumbing contractors treat every after-hours call as a potential emergency because they lack the information to make better decisions. AI solves that.

**How AI triaging works for plumbing calls:**

### Emergency Dispatch Indicators (Immediate Dispatch)
- **Active leaks:** Water currently spraying, flooding, or dripping continuously
- **No water situations:** Customer has ZERO water at any fixture
- **Burst pipes:** Visible pipe damage, water main breaks, supply line failures
- **Sewage backups:** Toilets overflowing, drains backing up with sewage
- **Water heater flooding:** Tank ruptures, major leaks from water heaters
- **Flooding affecting electrical:** Water near outlets, appliances, or electrical panels

### Schedule for Next Available Slot (Non-Emergency)
- **Dripping faucets:** Slow drips, fixture leaks without active flooding
- **Slow drains:** Clogged drains that aren't causing backups
- **Weak water pressure:** Pressure issues without complete loss of water
- **Fixture repairs:** Leaky toilets, broken garbage disposals, faucet replacements
- **Water heater replacement:** Old units needing replacement (unless actively flooding)
- **Preventive maintenance:** Inspections, tune-ups, system upgrades

**Real example of AI triage in action:**

**Customer:** "My water heater isn't working."

**AI:** "I can help with that. Is the water heater actively leaking water onto the floor, or is it just not producing hot water?"

**Customer:** "No leaks, just no hot water for showers in the morning."

**AI:** "That sounds like a repair or replacement situation rather than an emergency. I can schedule a technician for tomorrow between 8-10 AM, or 12-2 PM. Which works better for you? They'll assess the unit and provide options."

This conversation saves your on-call technician from a 2 AM dispatch for a non-emergency, preserves their capacity for true emergencies, and still captures the job for your business instead of losing it to voicemail.

## Water Shut-Off Instructions: Containing Damage While Dispatching

One of the most valuable aspects of AI consulting for plumbing companies is something nobody else talks about: **AI providing water shut-off instructions during emergency dispatch.**

Here's why this matters: When a customer calls with a burst pipe or major leak, every minute that water flows equals more damage. More damage means higher repair costs, insurance claims, and unhappy customers. The fastest way to stop damage is to shut off the water—but most customers don't know how.

**AI delivers targeted, situation-specific water shut-off guidance:**

### Burst Pipe Scenario
**AI:** "While the technician is on the way, let's stop the water flow. Locate your main water shutoff valve—usually in the basement near the front wall, in a utility closet, or outside near the water meter. It's a wheel or lever. Turn the wheel clockwise, or move the lever perpendicular to the pipe. That should stop all water flow to your home."

### Water Heater Leaking Scenario
**AI:** "For water heater leaks, there's a shutoff valve on the cold water pipe going INTO the heater—usually on the right side as you face it. Turn that valve clockwise to stop water flow into the tank. If you can't find it or it won't turn, we'll shut off water at the main when the technician arrives."

### Fixture-Specific Leaks
**AI:** "Since this is a kitchen faucet leak, look under the sink for the small shut-off valves on the water lines—usually oval handles or small levers. Turn both clockwise to stop water to just that faucet. You'll still have water elsewhere in the house."

This isn't generic advice—AI provides specific guidance based on the type of plumbing emergency. Customers feel helped and supported. Damage is contained. Your technician arrives with the situation stabilized instead of worsening.

## Commercial vs. Residential Protocols: Different Rules for Different Buildings

Plumbing AI consulting addresses a critical distinction that generic solutions miss: **Commercial plumbing calls require different handling than residential calls.**

### Residential Plumbing Calls
- **Decision maker:** Usually the homeowner or tenant
- **Authority:** Can approve repair costs on the spot
- **Dispatch:** Send standard residential plumbing technician
- **Priority:** Triage based on severity (active leak vs. scheduled repair)

### Commercial Plumbing Calls
- **Decision maker:** Property manager, facilities manager, or business owner
- **Authority:** Often needs approval for work over $500-1,000
- **Dispatch:** Commercial plumbing technician with different equipment and expertise
- **Priority:** Commercial clients may have priority service contracts or different emergency protocols

**AI handles this distinction automatically:**

**AI:** "Is this for a residential property or a commercial building?"

**Customer:** "It's for our office building—we have 30 employees and no water in the building."

**AI:** "I understand this is a commercial emergency. Are you the property manager or facilities manager, or should I contact someone else for approval?"

**Customer:** "I'm the office manager, but the property manager handles repairs."

**AI:** "Can you provide the property manager's contact information? I'll need to get approval for emergency dispatch, or they can call me directly at this number. In the meantime, how many floors are affected and where is your main shutoff located?"

This conversation captures the right information, routes to the appropriate decision-maker, and ensures commercial plumbing protocols are followed.

Generic AI solutions that treat all calls the same end up dispatching residential techs to commercial jobs, or waste time trying to get approval from employees who don't have it. Plumbing-specific AI consulting builds these distinctions into the system.
