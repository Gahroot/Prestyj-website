## The Brokerage Appointment Setting Problem

A real estate brokerage's most valuable output is a booked appointment. Not a lead. Not a form submission. A qualified buyer or seller sitting across from one of your agents.

The challenge: there's a massive gap between "lead received" and "appointment booked."

In between, you need:
- Someone to respond immediately (within minutes, ideally seconds)
- Someone to have a qualifying conversation
- Someone to overcome objections ("I'm just looking")
- Someone to identify serious buyers vs. tire-kickers
- Someone to navigate calendar availability and book the meeting
- Someone to send reminders and handle reschedules

Historically, this was a human ISA's job. But ISAs are expensive, inconsistent, and they quit.

AI appointment setting handles every step of this process automatically — and does it better than most humans on the things that matter most (speed, consistency, coverage).

## How AI Appointment Setting Works for Brokerages

### Step 1: Lead Arrives

A lead submits a form from your Facebook ad, YouTube ad, or real estate website. The lead enters your CRM and simultaneously triggers your AI.

### Step 2: Instant AI Response (Under 60 Seconds)

AI sends a personalized SMS to the lead — referencing their specific inquiry, introducing your brokerage, and asking an opening qualifying question.

Example (for a home buyer lead):
> "Hi Sarah! This is Alex with Prestige Realty. I saw you were looking at homes in the Westside area — exciting stuff! What's most important to you in your next home?"

This message arrives within 30–60 seconds of the lead submitting. While they're still on their phone. While your ad is still fresh.

### Step 3: AI Qualification Conversation

AI conducts the qualification conversation via SMS. It asks about:
- Buyer vs. seller status
- Timeline (when are you looking to move?)
- Price range
- Pre-approval status (for buyers)
- Motivation to move
- Preferred neighborhoods/property types

This isn't a rigid questionnaire — it's a natural back-and-forth conversation. AI adapts its questions based on what the lead says.

### Step 4: Hot Lead Routing

When a lead meets your "hot" criteria (e.g., pre-approved buyer wanting to move in 90 days), AI immediately routes them to the appropriate agent with a full conversation summary.

The agent receives a notification like:
> "🔥 Hot lead: Sarah Johnson, buyer, pre-approved up to $450K, 60-day timeline, interested in 3-bed in Westside. She's ready to set up a consultation."

### Step 5: Appointment Booking

AI offers the lead available time slots from the agent's calendar and books the appointment directly. No phone calls required. No back-and-forth emails. The lead picks a time, AI confirms, and sends reminders.

### Step 6: Nurture for Non-Hot Leads

Leads who aren't ready yet (6–12+ month timeline, early research phase) go into a nurture sequence — with AI following up weekly or monthly until their timing changes.

## What to Look for in AI Appointment Setting for Your Brokerage

### Multi-Agent Routing

For brokerages with multiple agents, AI must be able to route leads to the right agent based on:
- Round-robin distribution
- Territory/geography
- Buyer vs. seller specialty
- Agent availability
- Price point

Look for AI that lets you customize routing rules and adjust them as your team evolves.

### CRM Integration

AI appointment setting is only as good as its integration with your CRM. Ensure the platform integrates with:
- Follow Up Boss
- kvCORE
- Sierra Interactive
- CINC
- Real Geeks
- Any CRM via Zapier

All conversations, lead notes, and appointments should sync automatically.

### Calendar Integration

AI must connect to each agent's calendar (Google Calendar, Outlook, Cal.com) to offer accurate availability. Appointments should block on both the AI side and the agent's actual calendar.

### Compliance Features

Real estate is subject to fair housing laws. Ensure your AI platform:
- Doesn't discriminate based on protected characteristics
- Maintains conversation logs for compliance
- Has been tested for fair housing compliance

### Performance Reporting

Brokers need visibility into:
- Response times per agent
- Appointment rates by lead source
- Conversion rates across campaigns
- Revenue attribution
