## The Solo Founder's Real Problem

You're CEO, head of sales, head of marketing, head of customer success, and the person closing the laptop at 11pm. You need video ads because:

But you have:

- 2–6 hours/week realistically available for marketing production
- A budget that breaks at $5k/mo in creative spend
- No team to manage freelancers
- No editor on retainer

Most "video ad creator" tools and services assume you have at least one of those. Solo founders need options that don't.

## Common Solo Founder Mistakes With Ads

Five mistakes that burn solo founder ad budgets:

1. **Filming "the perfect ad" once.** A perfect ad fatigues in 2–3 weeks. The solo founder who spends three weekends filming one polished ad has built an asset with a 2-month shelf life.
2. **Going wide on creative tools.** Trying Arcads, HeyGen, Pictory, Captions.ai, and Veed simultaneously means none of them get mastered. Pick one tool category and commit.
3. **Ignoring captions and hook overlays.** Even great founders skip these. 80% of mobile viewers watch with sound off; captions aren't optional.
4. **Running ads without a clear next-step.** No Calendly link, no application, no opt-in. The ad does its job; the funnel doesn't.
5. **Optimizing too early.** Solo founders often try to optimize CPL in week 2. The first 30 days are for learning what hooks work. Optimization comes after learning.

## What a Solo Founder Can Actually Film Themselves

For founders who want to film some content but not all of it, the highest-leverage format mix:

- **One 30-min "interview yourself" recording per month** — talk through 10 customer questions on camera. Becomes 30+ clips.
- **2–3 Loom recordings per week** — demo your product, walk through a screen, react to a market event. Each becomes 4–8 clips.
- **5–10 phone-video moments per month** — walk-and-talks, customer reactions, behind-the-scenes. Stitches into reel-style content.

That's roughly 4 hours of founder filming per month producing 60–120+ raw moments — enough source material for a batch service to multiply into hundreds of variants.
