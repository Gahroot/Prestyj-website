Picture this: you just hired your third ISA in two years. The first one took four months to ramp up, started hitting her numbers in month five, then left for a competing team in month eight. There is. And thousands of real estate teams are already making the switch.

## The Real Cost of a Human ISA

Every team leader knows the sticker price of an ISA hire, but few account for the true, loaded cost. Let's break it down honestly.

A competent human ISA in most markets commands a base salary between $40,000 and $55,000, plus commission or bonuses that push total compensation to $50,000-$70,000 annually. On top of that, you are looking at payroll taxes, health insurance, and PTO that add another 20-30% to the base. Then there is the cost nobody talks about: ramp time. Most ISAs need three to six months before they are fully productive. During that window, you are paying full salary for half output.

And turnover? Industry data shows the average ISA tenure is just 12-18 months. Every departure means you restart that expensive cycle from scratch — recruiting, interviewing, onboarding, training, and waiting.

Add it all up and a single human ISA costs your team $70,000-$90,000 per year when you factor in the hidden expenses. For a team doing $10 million in volume, that is a significant line item.

## What an AI ISA Actually Does

If you haven't looked at AI ISA technology in the last year, throw out everything you think you know. Modern AI inside sales agents are nothing like the clunky chatbots of 2023. Here is what a purpose-built AI ISA for real estate handles today:

**Instant lead response.** When a lead comes in at 2 AM on a Saturday, your AI ISA responds within seconds — not minutes, not hours. Research consistently shows that responding within five minutes makes you 21 times more likely to qualify a lead compared to waiting 30 minutes. Your human ISA is asleep at 2 AM. Your AI never is.

**Qualifying conversations.** Through natural text and voice interactions, AI ISAs ask the right questions — timeline, budget range, pre-approval status, neighborhood preferences — and score leads based on their answers. No script fumbling, no awkward pauses, no forgetting to ask about financing.

**Appointment booking.** Qualified leads get routed directly to your agents' calendars. The AI handles scheduling, confirmations, and reminders without a single manual touchpoint.

**Follow-up sequences.** The leads who aren't ready today still get nurtured. AI ISAs execute persistent, personalized follow-up over weeks and months — the exact type of tedious, repetitive work that causes human ISAs to burn out and cut corners.

**Lead scoring and CRM updates.** Every interaction is logged, scored, and synced to your CRM automatically. No more chasing your ISA to update their notes.

## The ROI Math That Changes Everything

Let's run a scenario that most team leaders will recognize.

**Human ISA scenario:** Your ISA works 8 hours a day, 5 days a week. After accounting for breaks, admin work, and the natural pace of phone conversations, she books around 15 qualified appointments per month on a good month. At a fully loaded cost of $65,000 per year, that is roughly $5,417 per month — or **$361 per booked appointment**.

**AI ISA scenario:** Your AI ISA works around the clock, handling every inbound lead instantly and running follow-up sequences on hundreds of contacts simultaneously. It books around 40 qualified appointments per month because it never misses a lead and never stops following up. At $500 per month, that is **$12.50 per booked appointment**.

Read that again. $361 per appointment versus $12.50 per appointment. Even if you cut the AI's performance in half and double the human's, the AI still wins by a factor of ten.

If even one of those extra 25 monthly appointments converts to a closed deal, you have paid for the AI ISA for the entire year.

## What Human ISAs Still Do Better

We would be doing you a disservice if we pretended AI handles everything. It doesn't. There are situations where a skilled human ISA genuinely outperforms any AI on the market:

**Complex negotiations and objection handling.** When a lead has layered concerns — maybe they are going through a divorce, dealing with an inherited property, and worried about capital gains — a skilled human ISA can navigate that emotional complexity in ways AI cannot fully replicate yet.

**In-person relationship building.** Open houses, networking events, community meetups — these are human domains. The rapport built through a handshake and eye contact still matters in real estate.

**High-empathy situations.** Relocation after a job loss. Selling a family home after a death. These conversations require genuine emotional intelligence that no AI should attempt to fake.

**Strategic thinking.** A great ISA can read between the lines, pick up on subtle cues in conversation, and make judgment calls about when to push and when to pull back.

Here is the thing, though: these situations represent maybe 20% of an ISA's workload. The other 80% — the initial outreach, the speed-to-lead response, the repetitive qualification questions, the follow-up reminders, the scheduling back-and-forth — is exactly the type of work AI was built to handle.

## The Hybrid Model: Why You Don't Have to Choose

This is where smart teams are landing, and it is the approach we champion at Prestyj: **AI handles the volume, humans handle the nuance.**

Here is how the hybrid model works in practice:

1. **AI handles first contact.** Every new lead gets an instant response from your AI ISA. No leads slip through the cracks, regardless of time or day.

2. **AI qualifies and scores.** Through conversational exchanges, the AI determines timeline, motivation, budget, and readiness. Hot leads get flagged immediately.

4. **AI continues nurturing the rest.** The leads who aren't ready get placed into intelligent follow-up sequences. The AI keeps them warm until they are ready to re-engage.

## "But Leads Can Tell It's AI"

This is the objection we hear most often, and it made sense three years ago. It doesn't hold up anymore.

Modern conversational AI uses natural language processing that handles slang, typos, context switching, and even humor. The interactions feel like texting with a knowledgeable, responsive person — because the AI has been trained on millions of real estate conversations.

But here is the more important point: **leads care far more about speed than about who is responding.**

Think about your own behavior. When you fill out a form online, do you care whether the response comes from a person or a system? Or do you care that someone got back to you quickly with useful information? The data is overwhelming — fast response wins. A lead that hears back in 30 seconds is dramatically more likely to engage than one who waits 30 minutes for a human to call back.

The teams getting the best results are transparent about it. A simple "Hi, I'm the AI assistant for [Team Name]" followed by genuinely helpful, fast interaction builds more trust than radio silence while your human ISA finishes lunch.

## When to Make the Switch

You don't need to fire your ISA tomorrow. But you should seriously evaluate an AI ISA if any of these sound familiar:

- You are spending more than $4,000 per month on ISA compensation
- Your speed-to-lead time is consistently over 5 minutes
- You have lost an ISA to turnover in the past 18 months
- Your ISA's follow-up sequences are inconsistent or incomplete
- You are generating more leads than your team can handle
- Weekend and evening leads go unanswered until Monday morning

If you checked even two of those boxes, the math is already working against you.
