## AI Lead Qualification for HVAC: The Right Questions in the Right Order

The difference between a $200 repair and a $9,000 replacement is buried in 60 seconds of qualifying conversation. AI lead response systems built for HVAC know which questions to ask — and in what order — to route every lead correctly from the first contact.

### The HVAC Qualification Framework

**Step 1: Urgency Triage (first 15 seconds)**

Before anything else, AI determines whether this is a true emergency requiring immediate dispatch or a schedulable job.

Emergency signals that trigger immediate routing:
- "No AC" + indoor temp above 85°F + elderly/infant/medical conditions present
- "No heat" + outdoor temp below freezing + health vulnerability
- Gas smell from furnace (immediate safety dispatch, every time)
- Commercial walk-in cooler failure with perishable inventory at risk
- Carbon monoxide alarm in conjunction with HVAC issues

Urgent-but-schedulable signals:
- "AC isn't keeping up" or "not cooling well" — comfort issue, not emergency
- "Thermostat not responding" — often a simple fix
- "Strange noise" or "burning smell" — needs same-day attention but not emergency dispatch

**Step 2: System Age Qualification (seconds 15–30)**

System age is the single best predictor of repair vs. replacement intent. AI asks it early:

*"How old is your AC/heating system — do you know approximately?"*

| System Age | What AI Does |
|------------|-------------|
| Under 8 years | Routes to repair tech. Replacement unlikely unless major component failure. |
| 8–12 years | Flags for tech assessment. Could go either way depending on component. |
| 12–15 years | Flags replacement conversation. Tech arrives with replacement info ready. |
| 15+ years | Routes to replacement specialist or schedules comfort advisor. Captures budget signals. |
| Unknown | Asks follow-up: "Is it original to the house, or was it replaced at some point?" |

**Step 3: Problem Description (seconds 30–45)**

*"What's happening with the system — is it not running at all, running but not cooling, or something else?"*

This separates:
- **Compressor/full system failure** (potentially terminal; replacement conversation)
- **Refrigerant leak** (repair job; recurs on aging systems)
- **Capacitor/fan failure** (common repair; good prognosis)
- **Thermostat issue** (usually minor)
- **Ductwork/airflow** (could be separate from unit condition)

**Step 4: Residential vs. Commercial Routing (if not already clear)**

*"Is this for your home or a business?"*

Commercial leads get immediate re-routing:
- Different tech skill set required
- Different pricing (commercial emergency rates)
- Business urgency factors (inventory, customer impact, revenue loss)
- Often requires commercial-licensed equipment

**Step 5: Appointment or Dispatch**

Based on steps 1–4, AI routes to one of:
- **Immediate on-call dispatch** with tech notified and full context delivered
- **Same-day appointment** booked directly into ServiceTitan/Housecall Pro
- **Replacement consultation** with comfort advisor, including home size and system details collected
- **Scheduled maintenance** during next available tech window
