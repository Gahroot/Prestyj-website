## What Counts as a Dormant Real Estate Lead?

For reactivation purposes, "dormant" typically means:

- **Portal inquiries** (Zillow, Realtor.com, Redfin) older than 90 days with no recent agent engagement
- **Open-house signups** that never converted to a showing or transaction
- **Expired follow-up sequences** in Follow Up Boss / kvCORE
- **Past clients** outside their active transaction window who haven't been touched in 12+ months

A typical 5-agent team accumulates 3,000–12,000 dormant contacts in 18–36 months. Most have measurable re-intent value — they were real leads, the moment just passed.

## How Multi-Channel AI Reactivation Works

1. **Segment the dormant database.** Pull contacts ≥90 days inactive with valid phone + email.
2. **Day 1: SMS soft re-open.** Branded sender ID, short message tied to a relevant local market trigger.
3. **Day 2–3: AI voice agent dial** under local-time compliance windows, with branded caller ID.
4. **Day 4: Email follow-up** with a single CTA (free home valuation, market report, or showing request).
5. **Day 7–14: Re-engagement loop** for non-responders with a different angle.
6. **Live transfer or appointment booking** for everyone who engages → back into the agent's CRM workflow.

Expect 60–75% of the engaged conversions to happen in the first 10 days of the campaign.

## What "6–14% Conversion" Actually Means in Dollars

Take a brokerage with a 5,000-contact dormant database and a $14,000 average gross commission. A 30-day reactivation campaign:

- 5,000 contacts × 10% live-conversation rate = 500 conversations
- 500 conversations × 6% appointment rate = 30 appointments
- 30 appointments × 30% close rate (over 90 days) = 9 closed transactions
- 9 × $14,000 GCI = **$126,000 in recovered commission**

At typical reactivation pricing ($0.60/contact = $3,000 total campaign cost), the ROI is roughly 40×. Even halving the assumptions, the math holds.
