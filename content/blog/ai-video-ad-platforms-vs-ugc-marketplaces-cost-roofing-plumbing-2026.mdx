## Key Takeaways

- **AI video / batch cost:** $5–$95 per finished contractor ad depending on self-serve vs managed workflow.
- **UGC marketplace cost:** $300–$900+ per finished ad after revisions, usage rights, reshoots, and platform fees.
- **Cost per tested angle:** Batch video can test an angle for [$400–$500](/stat/bva-cost-per-tested-angle); UGC often costs $700–$3,500+ per angle.
- **Roofing AI fit:** Storm inspection hooks, financing, insurance myths, urgency, warranty objections, neighborhood proof variants.
- **Plumbing AI fit:** Emergency calls, water heaters, drain cleaning, repipes, maintenance, “what it costs” explainers.
- **UGC still matters:** Real jobsite footage, before/after proof, technician trust, customer testimonial, and local credibility.
- **Best stack:** 80–90% AI/batch creative volume + 10–20% real UGC proof assets.

## Roofing: Which Model Works Best?

Roofing is trust-heavy and high-ticket. Homeowners want proof that the contractor is local, insured, and competent. But roofing also needs a lot of creative volume because storm timing, neighborhood, insurance language, financing, and skepticism all change performance.

| Roofing angle | AI/batch fit | UGC fit | Best recommendation |
|---|---|---|---|
| Storm inspection urgency | Excellent | Medium | AI batch variants by neighborhood and storm date |
| Insurance claim myths | Excellent | Medium | AI batch scripts with human review |
| Before/after roof proof | Medium | Excellent | Real UGC/jobsite footage |
| Owner/founder trust story | High | High | Batch clips from owner recording |
| Financing / monthly payment | Excellent | Low–Medium | AI batch offer variants |
| Hail damage education | Excellent | Medium | AI batch plus real damage footage |
| Customer testimonial | Medium | Excellent | Real customer UGC |
| Warranty comparison | High | Medium | AI batch explainer variants |

A roofing company should not try to fake jobsite authenticity with AI. Use AI for message volume and real footage for proof.

## What Vendors Don’t Tell You

1. **UGC creator marketplace costs are rarely all-in.** Usage rights, revisions, reshoots, and exclusivity can add [22–48%](/stat/ugc-marketplace-hidden-cost-percent).
2. **Self-serve AI tools still require labor.** Someone has to write hooks, edit scripts, QA outputs, and format assets.
3. **Roofing and plumbing need local trust.** Generic creator content can underperform if it does not feel trade-specific.
4. **One great testimonial is not a testing system.** It can be a hero asset, but it will fatigue.
5. **AI batch does not remove the need for real footage.** It reduces how much new footage you need to test messages.

## FAQ

### Are AI video ad platforms cheaper than UGC marketplaces for roofing and plumbing?

Yes. AI video and batch video workflows often cost **$5–$95 per finished ad**, while UGC marketplace ads usually cost **$300–$900+** once revisions, usage, and platform fees are included.

### Do AI video ads work for contractors?

They work best for testing hooks, offers, objections, seasonal messages, financing angles, and emergency-service variations. They should be paired with real jobsite proof, technician footage, and testimonials.

### Should roofers use AI video ads or UGC creators?

Roofers should use both. Use AI/batch for storm, financing, insurance, warranty, and neighborhood-angle testing. Use UGC or in-house footage for real roof proof, customer testimonials, and technician credibility.

### Should plumbers use AI video ads or UGC creators?

Plumbers can lean heavier into AI batch because many plumbing problems are repeatable: emergency leaks, drains, water heaters, repipes, sewer, and maintenance. UGC is still useful for trust and proof.

### What is cost per tested angle?

Cost per tested angle is the cost to test one customer problem with enough ad variations to learn whether it works. For batch video, this can be around [$400–$500](/stat/bva-cost-per-tested-angle); for UGC and agency workflows it can be much higher.

### Why not just buy 5 high-quality UGC ads?

Five ads may all lose because creative performance has high variance. Batch testing improves the odds by testing many hooks, bodies, CTAs, and angles rather than betting everything on a few assets.

### Will AI video ads look fake?

They can if generated carelessly. A managed batch workflow should use realistic formats, clear scripts, human QA, brand review, and real footage where credibility matters.

### What is the best monthly creative mix for contractors?

A strong starting point is 80–90% AI/batch variants and 10–20% real UGC or jobsite proof assets. The exact mix depends on ad spend, service line, seasonality, and proof library.

### How many video ads do roofing or plumbing companies need per month?

Small accounts may need 25–50 new variants per month. Growing paid social accounts often need 100–300+ monthly variants to keep testing and avoid fatigue.

### What should contractors test first?

Start with emergency vs planned service, price vs speed, trust proof vs warranty, financing vs cash price, owner-led vs technician-led, and seasonal urgency angles.
