## The Three Categories of AI Voice Agent Platforms

### 1. Managed / Done-For-You (Best for Non-Technical Teams)

**Prestyj, Air.ai, Goodcall**

These platforms handle everything: setup, conversation design, CRM integration, ongoing optimization, and support. You don't write code, configure telephony, or manage LLM prompts. You describe your use case and they deploy it.

**When to choose:** You want results (booked appointments, answered calls, qualified leads), not a technology project. Your team doesn't have AI engineers. You value speed-to-deployment (days, not months).

**Cost profile:** Higher per-minute rate but lower 12-month TCO because engineering costs ($0 vs $20,000–$60,000) dwarf the per-minute difference.

### 2. DIY No-Code Platforms (Best for Semi-Technical Teams)

**Synthflow**

These platforms provide visual builders and templates so you can configure voice agents without writing code. You still need to understand conversation flows, API integrations, and telephony basics — but no software engineering.

**When to choose:** You have a technical-minded team member (ops manager, IT generalist) who can invest 20–40 hours in setup. You want more control than managed platforms offer but can't justify a developer.

**Cost profile:** Lower per-minute rate than managed. Moderate setup time. Ongoing maintenance requires 5–15 hours/month.

### 3. DIY Developer Platforms (Best for Engineering Teams)

**Vapi, Retell AI, Bland AI, LiveKit, ElevenLabs**

Raw infrastructure for building voice agents. You get APIs, SDKs, and documentation. You write the code, configure telephony, manage LLM prompts, handle edge cases, and maintain the system.

**When to choose:** You have a dedicated AI engineer or development team. You need maximum customization (custom models, proprietary integrations, unique use cases). You're building voice AI into a product you sell.

**Cost profile:** Lowest per-minute rate. Highest hidden cost: $20,000–$60,000/year in engineering time for a production-grade system. A single engineer departure can leave your voice system unmaintained.

## The Hidden Cost Check

Before signing with any platform, ask:

1. What is the fully-loaded per-minute cost at my expected volume? (Not the advertised rate.)
2. What's the total setup cost including engineering time? (At $100/hour.)
3. Are telephony, LLM, STT, and TTS included or separate?
4. What's the ongoing maintenance cost in time and money?
5. What happens if my engineer leaves? (DIY platforms only.)

A platform that won't give you fully-loaded numbers is hiding something.

## FAQ

**What's the cheapest AI voice agent platform?**
The cheapest advertised rate is Vapi at ~$0.05/minute. The cheapest fully-loaded platform is Prestyj at $0.25–$0.50/minute for managed, or Synthflow at $0.15–$0.35/minute for DIY — because you don't pay for engineering time.

**Vapi vs Retell vs Bland — which is best?**
All three are developer platforms with similar pricing. Vapi has the best documentation. Retell has superior conversation flow control. Bland is optimized for high-volume outbound. All require 40–80 hours of engineering setup.

**Should I choose managed or DIY?**
If you don't have a dedicated AI engineer on staff, choose managed. The $20,000–$60,000/year in engineering overhead for DIY platforms exceeds the per-minute savings versus managed platforms.

**How accurate are the advertised per-minute rates?**
They're typically [18–35% lower](/stat/voice-agent-hidden-cost-percent) than what you'll actually pay once telephony, LLM inference, STT/TTS, and overage are included.
