## What "Done-For-You" Actually Means for Home Services

Most vendors throw around "done-for-you" like it means push-button magic. Here's the transparency you won't find on sales calls:

**Done-for-you means the vendor handles:**
- Strategy and workflow design (what questions the AI asks, how it routes calls)
- Technical implementation (phone system integration, CRM connection, AI voice setup)
- Conversation flow building (not just templates, but your specific business logic)
- Testing and quality assurance (finding edge cases before customers do)
- Ongoing optimization (refining based on real conversations)

**Done-for-you does NOT mean:**
- You do nothing (you still need to provide business knowledge, approval, and feedback)
- It works perfectly out of the box (even turnkey solutions require your input)
- Results are guaranteed (your reputation, service area, and pricing still matter)
- No technical involvement ever (you'll need to provide access to systems, approve integrations)

**What vendors actually need from you:**
- Access to your CRM or scheduling system (ServiceTitan, Jobber, Housecall Pro, etc.)
- Your service area boundaries and any territory rules for technicians
- Emergency vs. routine call definitions (what warrants immediate dispatch?)
- Pricing ranges for common services (so AI can set appropriate expectations)
- Approval of conversation scripts and call flows before launch
- 4-8 hours of your time across the implementation process

If a vendor tells you "zero time investment" or "no involvement needed," that's a red flag. Even done-for-you requires your business expertise to work well.

## How Home Service AI Implementation Actually Works

Here's what the process looks like from a vendor perspective—timelines, milestones, and where things typically go wrong.

### Phase 1: Discovery and Strategy (Week 1)

**What happens:** The vendor learns your business inside and out. Not just "you're a plumbing company" but the specifics that make your operation unique.

**What they need from you:**
- Call recordings or examples of how your best staff handles calls
- Your emergency dispatch protocol (who gets notified, how)
- Service area map and any technician territory assignments
- Average job values and typical appointment lengths
- Your current tech stack (CRM, phone system, scheduling software)

**What they deliver:** Implementation plan with conversation flows mapped out, integration architecture, and timeline.

**Red flag watch:** Vendors who skip discovery and promise to "figure it out as we go" will waste months of your time. Every home service business is different—your emergency protocols, service territory rules, and appointment scheduling are unique.

### Phase 2: Build and Integration (Week 2-4)

**What happens:** The technical work. AI agents are built, phone systems are connected, CRM integrations are established.

**What they need from you:**
- Test credentials for your CRM/scheduling system
- Access to your phone system or willingness to port numbers
- Approval of conversation scripts (how AI introduces itself, questions it asks)
- Feedback on initial call flows (does this match how your business operates?)

**What they deliver:** Working AI agents integrated with your systems, ready for testing.

**Red flag watch:** Vendors who can't show you the actual conversation script or who refuse your edits to how AI speaks. This is your brand—AI represents you. If they're protective of "their proprietary scripts," walk away.

### Phase 3: Testing and Refinement (Week 4-6)

**What happens:** Internal testing before live deployment. Finding edge cases, refining responses, ensuring emergency dispatch works.

**What they need from you:**
- 2-4 hours testing the AI yourself (call it, try to break it, find issues)
- Feedback on edge cases (what happens when someone asks X?)
- Approval of escalation rules (when should AI transfer to a human?)

**What they deliver:** Refined AI agents, documented escalation protocols, go-live readiness.

**Red flag watch:** Vendors who want to go live without adequate testing "to see how it performs in production." That's your business they're experimenting on. Testing happens first, always.

### Phase 4: Launch and Optimization (Week 6-8+)

**What happens:** Gradual rollout, real-world monitoring, rapid iteration based on actual calls.

**What they need from you:**
- 1-2 hours/week reviewing call summaries and metrics
- Feedback on what's working and what isn't
- Approval for significant changes to call flows

**What they deliver:** Live AI handling your calls, weekly reports, optimization recommendations.

**Reality check:** AI doesn't stop improving after launch. The first 4-6 weeks are about refinement. Vendors who "set it and forget it" aren't providing done-for-you—they provided a one-time setup and called it done.

## Home Services Use Cases: HVAC, Plumbing, Roofing

Different trades have different needs. Here's how done-for-you AI agents work for specific home service industries.

### HVAC Companies: Emergency vs. Routine Triaging

**The challenge:** Every HVAC call could be emergency (no heat in freezing temps, no AC during heatwave) or routine (maintenance, filter replacement, system upgrade). Dispatching a tech for routine calls wastes capacity; missing emergencies destroys reputation.

**How AI handles it:**
- **Urgency detection:** AI asks about temperature, timeline, and whether they have *any* heating/cooling
- **Emergency dispatch:** No heat below 40°F or no AC above 95°F triggers immediate dispatch to on-call tech
- **Routine scheduling:** Maintenance and upgrades get scheduled in available slots with clear pricing expectations
- **Service area verification:** Confirms address before dispatching techs to profitable jobs only

**Real example:**
*"Customer calls at 11 PM in February: "My furnace isn't working."*

**AI response:** "I'm sorry to hear that—is your home currently below 50 degrees, or is it just not reaching your usual temperature?"

**Customer:** "It's 45 degrees and dropping fast."

**AI action:** Flags as emergency, verifies address in service area, confirms emergency pricing applies, dispatches on-call tech with ETA, texts customer confirmation—under 90 seconds total.

### Plumbing Companies: Active Leak vs. Scheduled Repair

**The challenge:** Plumbing emergencies (burst pipes, active flooding, no water) require immediate dispatch. Routine jobs (faucet repair, toilet installation, water heater replacement) can wait. Dispatching emergency techs for non-emergencies kills profitability.

**How AI handles it:**
- **Emergency triage:** Asks about active water flow, flooding, whether ANY fixtures work
- **Active leak dispatch:** Burst pipes, major leaks, no water situations trigger emergency response
- **Service area enforcement:** Verifies zip code/address before dispatching—no wasted trips
- **Job qualification:** Captures problem type, property type (residential/commercial), and urgency to determine appropriate response

**Real example:**
*"Customer calls at 3 AM: "My water heater is leaking."*

**AI response:** "I can help with that. Is this an active leak where water is currently flowing onto the floor, or more of a drip from the tank or pipes?"

**Customer:** "It's actively leaking. There's already an inch of water on the floor."

**AI action:** Flags as active emergency, confirms address, dispatches emergency plumber with wet-vac capability, texts customer "On the way within 60 minutes" with emergency pricing disclosure.

### Roofing Contractors: Storm Surge and Insurance Claims

**The challenge:** Storms create call surges (50-200 calls in hours) that overwhelm human staff. Insurance vs. cash jobs require different handling. Fast response determines whether you get the $15,000-25,000 replacement job.

**How AI handles it:**
- **Unlimited concurrent calls:** Handles 100+ simultaneous calls during storm surges
- **Insurance capture:** Collects insurance carrier, claim number, adjuster contact when applicable
- **Damage assessment:** Asks about visible damage, leaks, age of roof to prioritize inspections
- **Inspection scheduling:** Books roof inspections directly into estimator calendar with address and details

**Real example:**
*"Customer calls day after hailstorm: "I think the hail damaged my roof."*

**AI response:** "I'm sorry to hear that. Have you noticed any actual leaks inside the house, or is the damage primarily to the shingles outside?"

**Customer:** "No leaks yet, but there are shingles all over the yard and some look pretty bare."

**AI action:** Categories as non-emergency inspection needed, confirms address, asks "Are you filing this through insurance or planning to pay out of pocket?" captures insurance info if applicable, offers inspection slots for next 2 days, sends confirmation with what to expect during inspection.

## Red Flags: When AI Vendors Are Wasting Your Money

After analyzing hundreds of AI implementations, here are the warning signs that signal you're about to have a bad experience.

### Red Flag 1: Unrealistic Timeline Promises

**What they say:** "We'll have you live in 3 days."

**Why it's a red flag:** Even simple integrations take 2-4 weeks for quality implementation. Rushed implementations skip testing, skip your feedback, and go live with half-baked systems that hurt your brand.

**Realistic timeline:** 4-8 weeks for quality done-for-you implementation. Longer if you have complex integrations or unusual business rules.

### Red Flag 2: Vague Pricing Structures

**What they say:** "Pricing starts at $X/month" or "Pay per conversation."

**Why it's a red flag:** Per-conversation pricing gets expensive fast when calls surge (storm season for roofers, winter for HVAC). "Starting at" means the real price is 3-5x higher once they add all necessary features.

**What you want:** Flat monthly pricing with clear scope. "X/month covers unlimited conversations, these integrations, and this level of optimization."

### Red Flag 3: No Sample Conversations or Scripts

**What they say:** "Trust us, our AI is trained on millions of conversations."

**Why it's a red flag:** Every business is different. A plumbing company in rural Ohio handles calls differently than a plumbing company in urban Miami. If they won't show you exactly how the AI will speak to your customers, they're hiding something.

**What you want:** Sample scripts, sample call recordings, and the ability to edit and approve how AI represents your business.

### Red Flag 4: Limited Integration Experience

**What they say:** "We can integrate with anything" or "Integration is easy."

**Why it's a red flag:** ServiceTitan integrations are different from Jobber integrations, which are different from Housecall Pro. Vendors with deep experience in your specific CRM will anticipate edge cases and problems. Vendors learning on your dime will waste months.

**What you want:** "We've integrated with your CRM before. Here are case studies from similar HVAC/plumbing/roofing companies using ServiceTitan/Jobber/etc."

### Red Flag 5: No Ongoing Optimization Included

**What they say:** "We'll set you up and you're good to go."

**Why it's a red flag:** AI agents need continuous optimization based on real conversations. The first month always reveals edge cases no one anticipated. Vendors who include ongoing monitoring and adjustment get you better results. Vendors who don't leave you with a static tool that degrades.

**What you want:** "We monitor your calls weekly, identify improvement opportunities, and implement them automatically. Here's our process for optimization."

## Vendor Evaluation Framework: How to Choose

Use this framework when evaluating done-for-you AI vendors for your home services business.

### Technical Capabilities (30 points)

- **CRM integration experience (10):** Have they integrated with your specific system before?
- **Voice quality and latency (5):** Does the AI sound natural? Is there awkward delay?
- **Simultaneous call handling (5):** Can they handle surges (especially important for roofing)?
- **Emergency dispatch capability (10):** Can they flag and dispatch emergencies appropriately?

### Service and Support (30 points)

- **Implementation timeline (10):** Realistic 4-8 weeks or rushed "live in days"?
- **Ongoing optimization (10):** Continuous improvement or set-and-forget?
- **Account management (10):** Dedicated person or shared support queue?

### Business Fit (20 points)

- **Home services experience (10):** Do they understand HVAC/plumbing/roofing specifically?
- **Service area handling (5):** Can they enforce your territory boundaries?
- **Emergency vs. routine logic (5):** Can they triage appropriately for your trade?

### Pricing Transparency (20 points)

- **Clear flat pricing (10):** Predictable monthly or confusing per-call structures?
- **Hidden fees awareness (10):** Upfront about integration costs or surprise charges later?

**Score vendors on each category. Anything below 60/100 is a pass. 70+ is worth considering. 85+ is a strong candidate.**
