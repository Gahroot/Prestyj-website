## Key Takeaways

- **Compare cost per connected call, not per-line list price.** Coverage and per-display fees move the real number more than the headline fee does.
- **Per-line fees run [$12–$45/month](/stat/branded-calling-cost-per-line)**; fully-loaded cost per connected conversation runs [$0.04–$0.11](/stat/branded-calling-cost-per-connected-call) at 2,000+ dials/month.
- **The formula:** cost per connected call = (line fee + per-display fees) ÷ (dials × baseline connect rate × answer-rate lift).
- **A [27–41% answer-rate lift](/stat/branded-calling-answer-rate-lift)** is what converts a flat fee into a lower per-conversation cost.
- **Per-display micro-fees** ($0.005–$0.02/display) can flip the cheapest-on-paper vendor into the most expensive at scale.

## The Cost-Per-Call Formula

Don't compare $/line. Compare $/connected conversation:

```
Cost per connected call =
  (monthly line fee + monthly per-display fees)
  ÷ (monthly dials × baseline connect rate × (1 + answer-rate lift))
```

Two vendors at the same $30/line can produce very different cost-per-call numbers if one displays your brand on 80% of devices and the other on 55%. Coverage *is* the cost lever.

## Why the Cheapest List Price Often Loses

CallerID Reputation has the lowest line fee, and at low volume on a narrow carrier set it can genuinely be the cheapest per call. But scale up or shift toward mobile-heavy lists, and a provider with 80%+ coverage connects enough additional conversations that its *per-call* cost drops below the budget option — even at a higher line fee. List price tells you the floor; coverage tells you the real cost.

## Hidden Costs That Distort the Comparison

- **Per-display micro-fees** ($0.005–$0.02 each). At 10,000 displays/month that's $50–$200 the list price never showed you.
- **Tiered usage fees** (TNS) that scale with volume — model them at *your* projected dials, not the entry tier.
- **Attestation re-registration** when DIDs change carriers.
- **Coverage gaps** that quietly lower your realized answer-rate lift, raising true cost per call without changing the invoice.

## How to Run the Comparison Yourself

1. **Get each vendor's carrier-coverage map** for your target area codes.
2. **Pull your baseline connect rate** from your dialer (unbranded).
3. **Ask each vendor for a realistic answer-rate lift** on your carrier mix — not the marketing average.
4. **Plug into the formula** and rank by cost per connected call.
5. **Add per-display and usage fees** at your projected volume before deciding.
