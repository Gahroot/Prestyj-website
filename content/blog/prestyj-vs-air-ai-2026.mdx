Pick Air.ai if you're running heavy B2B outbound with a team that owns the playbook. Pick Prestyj if you want a vertical-tuned inbound + outbound agent answering leads this week.

## Hidden Costs Buyers Underestimate (Both Platforms)

We'd be lying if we said only Air.ai has hidden costs. Here are the gotchas on **both** sides:

### Air.ai — what isn't in the headline price

1. **Sales-ops headcount or fractional consultant.** Air.ai is a managed product, but the playbook is yours. Most successful deployments either have an internal sales-ops owner or hire a fractional consultant ($1,500–$5,000/mo) to own scripts and optimization.
2. **Tier cliffs.** Hit 5,001 minutes on the Professional plan and you're paying Business tier overage rates or jumping to the $1,299/mo subscription whether you needed all 15,000 minutes or not.
3. **Custom voice work.** Sounds-like-a-real-rep voices are gated to Business tier ($1,299/mo) and above. Starter and Professional get standard library voices.
4. **Compliance review.** TCPA outbound rules, state-by-state disclosure language, DNC scrubbing — Air.ai is infrastructure for the conversation, not your compliance layer. Plan on a legal review pass ($2,000–$8,000) before launching at any scale.
5. **CRM cleanup.** Air.ai's integrations work, but field mapping for a messy CRM is non-trivial. Budget 8–16 hours of sales-ops time for a clean HubSpot or Salesforce sync.

### Prestyj — what isn't in the headline price

1. **Ad spend (if you're using the batch video ads add-on).** Media spend is separate.
2. **Phone number porting.** Free in most states, but timeline is 7–14 business days from your current carrier.
3. **Premium tier features** (multi-office, custom workflows, white-label) require Growth or Scale tier.
4. **Custom industry scripts** outside our default verticals are scoped as one-time engagements.

The "honest" version: Prestyj is more predictable inside its verticals, Air.ai is more flexible across generic B2B sales. Pick based on which trade-off your team can live with.

## Common Mistakes Buyers Make (And How to Avoid Them)

We've watched dozens of operators evaluate Air.ai. The mistakes are remarkably consistent.

### Mistake 1: Buying the demo, not the deployment

The viral Air.ai demos are real — but they're shot under ideal conditions with a polished playbook. Real-world deployment requires:

- 4–8 weeks of script tuning against actual call data
- Voice-tone calibration for your brand
- Handling 20–40 vertical-specific objections that the generic templates don't cover
- Disposition logic and follow-up sequences that fit your CRM

Most teams budget a week and spend six. Plan accordingly.

### Mistake 2: Confusing conversation length with conversion

Air.ai's "10–15 minute average call" is impressive — but for real estate qualification, home services dispatch, and most inbound use cases, **you don't want a 12-minute call**. You want a 3-minute qualification that ends in a booked appointment. Long-conversation strength is a feature for B2B discovery, not a universally good thing.

### Mistake 3: Underestimating playbook ownership

Air.ai gives you a managed product, but the playbook is yours. If your team doesn't already have a documented, working sales script — with objection responses, qualification criteria, and disposition logic — the agent will execute a mediocre playbook efficiently. That's not the win you think it is.

### Mistake 4: Forgetting compliance is your problem

TCPA outbound rules, fair housing language for real estate, DNC scrubbing, state-specific disclosures — Air.ai's platform is infrastructure for the conversation, not your compliance layer. Regulated verticals (real estate, mortgage, insurance, healthcare) need either internal compliance ownership or a vendor that bakes the guardrails in. Prestyj is the second; Air.ai is the first.

### Mistake 5: Picking premium subscription pricing for steady, predictable volume

Air.ai's tiered subscription rewards teams whose volume aligns with the tier breakpoints (1k, 5k, 15k, 50k minutes). If your real volume is 7,000 minutes/month, you're either paying for 8,000 minutes you don't use on the Business tier or fighting overage rates on Professional. Flat-fee mid-market pricing (Prestyj $1,997/$3,497/$5,997) absorbs the swing without forcing the decision.
