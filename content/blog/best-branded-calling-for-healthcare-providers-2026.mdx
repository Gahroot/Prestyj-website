## Hidden Costs Healthcare Buyers Miss

- **Per-display micro-fees** ($0.005–$0.02 each) on a small number of providers — $80–$300/month at 10k+ dials/line.
- **BAA requirements** — if your calling vendor records or stores call audio, they need a signed Business Associate Agreement under HIPAA. Not all branded calling providers offer this.
- **Logo/branded-name approval delays** of 5–15 business days — plan pilots around it, especially if your practice has multiple DBAs or specialty names.
- **Per-carrier coverage gaps** — display may render on AT&T but not a regional carrier. Always ask for a carrier-coverage map before signing, especially if you serve a rural area with smaller carriers.

## When Branded Calling Is NOT Worth It for Healthcare

- **Inbound-only call centers.** Branded calling lifts *outbound* answer rates; inbound caller-ID reputation is a separate product.
- **Very low outbound volume** (under ~500 dials/month per line) — the break-even doesn't pencil.
- **Already running compliant local-presence dialing** that's delivering acceptable connect rates (though carriers increasingly throttle it).
- **Telehealth-only practices** with minimal outbound calling needs.

**Q: What's the cheapest branded calling for a small healthcare practice?**
A: CallerID Reputation at [$12–$22 per line per month](/stat/branded-calling-cost-per-line) with $0 setup. The tradeoff is narrower carrier coverage than First Orion or Hiya.

**Q: What does branded calling cost per call for a healthcare practice?**

**Q: Is branded calling HIPAA compliant?**
A: Branded calling displays your practice name and logo on the recipient's screen — no PHI is transmitted via the caller ID display itself. However, if your calling vendor records or stores call audio, you need a signed BAA. Ask vendors about HIPAA compliance before signing.
