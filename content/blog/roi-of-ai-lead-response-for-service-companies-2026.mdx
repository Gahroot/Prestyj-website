## The Response Time Problem in Home Services

Every service company knows the drill. A homeowner's AC dies in July. They fill out a form on your website, leave a voicemail, or send a message through Google Local Services. Then they wait.

Meanwhile, you're on a job, the office manager is handling a billing dispute, and the lead notification sits unread for **45 minutes**. By the time someone calls back, the homeowner has already booked with the company that answered in under two minutes.

### The Math Behind Response Time

The data on response time and conversion is unambiguous:

| Response Time | Conversion Rate | Relative Effectiveness |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Within 1 minute | 39.1% | **21x baseline** |
| 1–5 minutes | 16.1% | 10x baseline |
| 5–30 minutes | 8.5% | 4x baseline |
| 30–60 minutes | 3.1% | 1.7x baseline |
| 1–24 hours | 1.4% | **1x (baseline)** |
| 24+ hours | 0.5% | 0.3x baseline |

> **Key stat:** Companies that respond within 5 minutes are **21x more likely** to qualify a lead than those responding after 30 minutes. Source: Harvard Business Review / InsideSales.com study.

For a service company with a **$1,200 average ticket**, the difference between a 39% close rate (1-minute response) and a 1.4% close rate (1–24 hours) on 300 leads/month is:

- **1-minute response:** 117 closed jobs × $1,200 = **$140,400/month**
- **24-hour response:** 4 closed jobs × $1,200 = **$4,800/month**

That's a **$135,600/month gap** — or **$1.6M/year** — from response time alone.

### Why Service Companies Can't Keep Up

The problem isn't effort. It's structural:

- **Peak lead times don't match office hours.** HVAC leads spike on Monday mornings and after weather events. Plumbing leads spike evenings and weekends. Your ISR isn't answering at 9 PM on a Saturday.
- **Volume fluctuates.** A 100°F week doubles or triples AC leads. A single hailstorm floods roofing inquiries. Human reps can't flex to 3x volume overnight.
- **Job site reality.** The owner or office manager is busy. Leads pile up. Callbacks happen at 6 PM when the homeowner has already booked elsewhere.
- **After-hours bleed.** 35–45% of service company leads come in outside business hours. Without after-hours response, those leads go cold by morning.

## The ROI Formula: Plug in Your Numbers

Here's the exact formula to calculate ROI for your company:

### Step 1: Calculate Current Lost Revenue

```
Lost Revenue = Monthly Leads × (Benchmarked Close Rate – Your Current Close Rate) × Average Ticket
```

**Example (HVAC, 15-truck):**
- Monthly leads: 400
- Benchmarked close rate (5-min response): 22%
- Your current close rate: 11%
- Average ticket: $1,800
- **Lost revenue = 400 × (0.22 – 0.11) × $1,800 = $79,200/month**

### Step 2: Calculate AI System Cost

```
AI Cost = Monthly Platform Fee + (Monthly Leads × Per-Lead Cost)
```

**Example:**
- Monthly platform fee: $2,500
- Per-lead cost: $5
- **AI Cost = $2,500 + (400 × $5) = $4,500/month**

### Step 3: Calculate Monthly Net ROI

```
Net ROI = Recovered Revenue – AI System Cost
```

**Example:**
- **Net ROI = $79,200 – $4,500 = $74,700/month**
- **Annual Net ROI = $896,400**
- **ROI Multiple = $79,200 / $4,500 = 17.6x**

### Step 4: Calculate Break-Even

```
Break-Even (days) = (AI Monthly Cost / Average Ticket) × (30 / Monthly Leads)
```

**Simplified:** How many additional jobs does AI need to close to cover its cost? Then divide monthly lead volume by that number to find how many days until you've generated enough leads for one extra close.

If your average callback time is over 5 minutes, you're leaving money on the table. Most service companies think they respond quickly because they're *trying* — but the data says otherwise. Track your actual callback time for one week. If it's more than 5 minutes, AI will outperform.
