Pick Hootsuite Managed Services if you're an enterprise brand with multi-stakeholder approval chains, global compliance requirements, and 6-figure annual social budgets. Pick Prestyj if you're an SMB, owner-operator, or lean marketing team that needs daily multi-platform volume and a predictable monthly bill.

### Hootsuite Managed Services Pricing

Hootsuite Managed Services pricing is **not publicly listed** — every engagement is custom-quoted based on scope, platforms, posting volume, video requirements, social listening needs, and contract length. Based on publicly disclosed enterprise engagements, partner-channel deals, and former Hootsuite client reports, the realistic price band looks like this:

| Engagement tier      | Monthly price       | Typical scope                                                   |
| -------------------- | ------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Boutique managed     | $5,000–$7,000       | 1 strategist (shared), 20–30 posts/mo, 2 platforms              |
| Standard managed     | $7,000–$12,000      | Dedicated strategist + creative shared, 30–50 posts/mo, 3 platforms |

- Paid social ad spend or paid campaign management (separate quote — Hootsuite Social Advertising is sold as a separate add-on)
- High-volume short-form vertical video production (typically capped at 4–8 videos/month per platform)

## What Hootsuite Managed Services Does Really Well

Honest credit. Hootsuite's Managed Services is one of the most mature done-for-you social operations in the industry, and there are scenarios where nothing else fits as cleanly.

**1. Multi-stakeholder enterprise approval workflows.** When 14 stakeholders across legal, brand, regional marketing, and PR need to sign off on every post, Managed Services has the workflow infrastructure, the account team to herd cats, and the platform routing to handle it. **4. Compliance review for regulated industries.** Pharma, financial services, healthcare — verticals where every post needs legal review before publishing. Managed Services builds compliance gates into the production workflow. SMB tools assume the operator is the compliance reviewer.

**5. Long-running brand relationships.** Brands that have run Hootsuite Managed Services for 5+ years cite the institutional brand knowledge the strategist team accumulates as one of the harder things to replicate elsewhere.

**6. Hootsuite Enterprise software depth.** The bundled software (publishing, listening, analytics) is best-in-class for enterprise teams managing 50+ social accounts, with role-based permissions and audit trails.

## Common Mistakes Buyers Make at the Enterprise-Tier Comparison

After hundreds of conversations with SMBs and mid-market brands evaluating Hootsuite Managed Services, these are the patterns that cost the most money:

**1. Buying enterprise scope for SMB-shaped problems.** A 50-person real estate team doesn't have a Fortune 500 approval chain. Buying Managed Services for that profile pays for stakeholder management you don't have. Prestyj's flat-fee model fits the actual shape of the operation.

**2. Confusing "managed" with "high volume."** Managed Services means a human team handles your account. It does not mean high posting volume. The two are orthogonal — most managed engagements top out at 20–60 posts/month, not 1,500.

**3. Signing the 12-month annual without testing volume needs.** Most brands underestimate how much volume the algorithms actually require. By month 3 they realize 30 posts/month isn't moving the needle — but they're locked in for 12.

**4. Pricing out only the software, not the people.** Hootsuite's published software pricing ($249–$739/mo retail) is a small fraction of Managed Services pricing. The "people" component is 70–85% of the invoice.

**5. Assuming bundled software justifies the agency premium.** The Hootsuite Enterprise software bundled in is worth $1,500–$3,000/mo retail. The remaining $5,000–$17,000/mo is paying for the strategist + creative team + account manager. Evaluate that "people premium" on its own merits.

**6. Skipping the question: "what's my actual posting volume need?"** Most owner-operators in SMB verticals need 1,000+ posts/month to be competitive on algorithm reach. Almost no managed-services engagement at any agency operates at that volume.

## What Vendors Don't Tell You About Managed Services Pricing

**Annual commits don't get unwound cleanly.** Most Managed Services contracts have 30–60 day notice clauses but require you to fulfill the annual commitment. Mid-year cancellation typically isn't refundable.

**Scope creep adds 15–30% over the year.** Extra video, extra platforms, ad hoc creative briefs, crisis ops events — every one adds line items. Year-end true-up often lands $20K–$60K above the contracted base.

**Strategist turnover happens.** Account teams change roles every 12–18 months at most agencies and managed services. Your dedicated strategist in month 2 may not be the same person in month 14, and the institutional knowledge handoff is uneven.

**Video production has soft caps.** "Unlimited video" almost never means unlimited — there's a "reasonable use" clause around video minutes per month, and exceeding it triggers production add-ons at $1,500–$5,000 per major piece.

**Ad spend is typically separate.** Most Managed Services scopes include organic publishing only. Paid social campaign management is sold as a separate add-on at 10–20% of ad spend or a flat retainer.

**Reporting cadence drives time-to-decision slowly.** QBRs every 90 days means optimizations land quarterly, not weekly. SMB-velocity decision-making (test on Tuesday, scale on Friday) doesn't fit the cadence.

## FAQ
