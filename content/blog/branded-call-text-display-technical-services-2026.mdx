**TL;DR: Branded call text display can show a verified business name, logo, and call reason on supported mobile networks and devices. It is more capable than a phone number or legacy CNAM, but no provider should be judged on a blanket “works everywhere” promise. Technical services firms should compare displayed-call coverage, number limits, commitments, and qualified conversations—not only monthly price.**

**Direct answer:** Start with a 30-day branded-calling pilot on one customer-approved workflow, such as renewal reminders or scheduled support callbacks. Use a public small-volume plan when possible, record how many attempted calls actually displayed the brand, and expand only when the branded cohort produces more qualified conversations per 1,000 attempts than an unbranded holdout. If the same workflow also needs automated follow-up and booking, compare the standalone display cost with a managed [AI voice agent](/ai-voice-agents).

## What Is Branded Call Text Display?

Branded call text display is the information a recipient sees before answering an outbound call. Depending on the provider, network, device, and region, the display may include:

- the verified business name;
- a company logo;
- a reason for the call, such as “Support Callback” or “Account Renewal”;
- a verification indicator; and
- the originating phone number.

This is different from three commonly confused layers:

| Identity layer | Typical display | Main limitation |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Phone-number caller ID | The originating number | The recipient may not recognize it |
| CNAM | A short business or caller name | Delivery depends on downstream databases and call paths |
| Branded caller ID | Name, logo, and sometimes call reason | Display still varies by supported network, device, and region |

Hiya’s official pricing FAQ describes CNAM as a name field used mainly on landlines in the US and Canada, while branded caller ID can display a customized name, logo, and call reason on select mobile devices and carriers. Amazon’s documentation separately warns that the caller ID name configured in Amazon Connect is not guaranteed to survive every public telephone network path.

Sources: [Hiya Branded Call pricing and FAQ](https://www.hiya.com/products/connect/pricing) and [Amazon Connect outbound caller ID documentation](https://docs.aws.amazon.com/connect/latest/adminguide/queues-callerid.html).

## Does Branded Calling Display on Every Android and iPhone?

No. Treat mobile coverage as an observed rate, not a universal feature flag.

Hiya states that display depends on the recipient’s network, device, and region. Its current US pricing page lists Samsung, T-Mobile, and Google as network or device coverage points and says customers are charged only when branding displays. First Orion states that its INFORM branded calls display on all major US networks, but a buyer should still validate the exact handset and carrier mix used by its customers.

A useful vendor question is not “Do you support Android and iOS?” Ask instead:

1. What percentage of our last 30 days of destination numbers would be eligible for display?
2. Which carriers, device families, operating-system versions, and regions are excluded?
3. Is billing based on attempted calls, eligible calls, or calls where branding displayed?
4. Can reporting export attempted, displayed, answered, and connected calls by number?
5. What happens when a logo or call reason does not render—does the verified name remain?

The right provider is the one that produces the best measured coverage on your recipients, not the longest logo list on a sales page.

## Which Technical Services Workflows Are the Best Pilot?

Choose a workflow where the recipient has a clear reason to recognize the business.

### Scheduled Support Callbacks

Use a call reason such as “Support Callback” only when the customer requested or expects the call. Keep the originating number stable so customers can save and return it.

### Renewal and Account Reviews

A recognizable company name can reduce uncertainty when an account manager calls about a known contract, renewal, or service review. Keep sales and support reasons separate in reporting.

### Security and Service Notifications

Use concise, factual call reasons. Branded display should support an established notification process; it should not replace authentication inside the conversation.

### Warm Lead Follow-Up

Call people who requested information, then measure speed to first attempt, answer rate, qualified conversation rate, and appointment rate. A recognizable display cannot rescue poor consent, a stale lead, or an irrelevant offer.

## How Do You Test Display Reliability?

Use a randomized holdout so seasonality and list quality do not decide the result for you.

1. Select one workflow and one group of approved originating numbers.
2. Split comparable recipients into branded and unbranded cohorts.
3. Keep call window, script, agent, retry cadence, and list source constant.
4. Record attempted, eligible, displayed, answered, qualified, booked, opt-out, and complaint counts.
5. Compare **qualified conversations per 1,000 attempts** between cohorts.
6. Calculate `incremental qualified conversations = (branded rate − holdout rate) × branded attempts`.
7. Calculate `cost per incremental qualified conversation = total branded-calling cost ÷ incremental qualified conversations`.
8. Expand only when the lift covers the full cost and complaints do not increase.

A raw answer-rate increase is not enough. The call reason could attract more answers without producing more useful conversations. Revenue or qualified appointments are stronger decision metrics.

## What Makes a Branded-Calling Setup Reliable at High Volume?

- **Stable number ownership:** Use numbers the business owns or is authorized to present.
- **Accurate registration:** Keep business identity, campaign purpose, and originating-number records current.
- **Separate call reasons:** Do not mix support, billing, renewal, and sales traffic under one vague label.
- **Carrier-aware reporting:** Review display and blocking by destination network, not only in aggregate.
- **Frequency controls:** Suppress recipients who answered, opted out, or reached the retry limit.
- **Fallback handling:** Decide what the recipient sees when the rich logo or reason is unavailable.
- **Human escalation:** Route complaints, sensitive questions, and unusual account issues to a person.
- **Monthly reputation review:** Monitor blocking, spam labels, answer rates, and number changes.

Amazon recommends using a valid caller ID, maintaining clean contact data, monitoring number reputation, respecting customer contact preferences, and validating outbound changes as experiments. Those controls matter whether a person or an AI agent places the call.

Source: [Amazon Connect guidance for outbound calling reputation](https://docs.aws.amazon.com/connect/latest/adminguide/optimize-outbound-calling.html).

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is Branded Call Text Display the Same as STIR/SHAKEN?

No. STIR/SHAKEN authenticates information about the calling number. Branded calling is a presentation layer that can add a verified business name, logo, and call reason on supported devices and networks.

### Can a Business Choose Any Call Reason?

The available fields and approval rules depend on the provider. Use a truthful, specific reason that matches the actual workflow, and test what appears on the destination devices used by customers.

### What Is the Cheapest Public Test in This Guide?

First Orion publicly lists 250 branded calls for $31 per month. Hiya also starts at 250 calls, but its crawlable pricing page requires the live calculator for the corresponding monthly quote and lists a $25 one-time setup fee.

### What Metric Should Decide Whether to Expand?

Use incremental qualified conversations or booked appointments per 1,000 attempts, then compare their value with the full branded-calling cost. Display rate and answer rate are diagnostic metrics, not the final business outcome.
