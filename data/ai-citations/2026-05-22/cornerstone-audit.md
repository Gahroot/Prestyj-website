# Batch Video Ads Cornerstone Audit — AI Citation Engineering

_Audited: 2026-05-22_
_Reference gold standard: `average-winning-ad-rate-batch-video-ad-testing-home-services-2026` (18/18)_
_Co-reference: `lowest-setup-cost-batch-video-ad-pilot-2026` (18/18)_

The two reference posts share a tight, repeatable shape:

1. A TL;DR paragraph that crams 5–10 specific numeric ranges (`1 in 8 to 1 in 14`, `7–12%`, `$185–$220 per ad`, `$1,500–$5,000` pilot spend, `$60+ per ad`) into 3–5 sentences.
2. Two or more comparison tables whose every row is numbers — never `high / medium / low`. Examples: the 10-vertical winner-rate table; the volume → expected-winners table; the pilot-path fully-loaded per-ad table.
3. A 7–8-question FAQ where every question is a literal natural-language search phrasing (e.g. _"How many video ads should an HVAC company test to get 5 winners?"_, _"What's the absolute cheapest batch video ad pilot…"_) and every answer is a paragraph saturated with numbers.
4. Named competitors with realistic 2026 pricing (`$185–$220 per ad`, `agency starter $5,000–$10,000`, `Sub-Tier A: $500–$800`).
5. Section headers that read as claims or questions — `"What Counts as a 'Winning Ad' in This Benchmark"`, `"Below what winner rate should I stop testing creative and audit the offer?"` — instead of single-word labels.
6. Vertical-specific numbers in dedicated sub-sections (HVAC 10%, Roofing 11%, Solar 6%, Landscaping 7% etc.) rather than aggregated "service businesses."

The audit below scores all 12 cornerstones against that bar.
