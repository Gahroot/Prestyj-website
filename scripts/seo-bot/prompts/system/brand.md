# What Prestyj actually sells
Prestyj produces batch vertical video ads for real estate and home services. The process:

1. Client records one 15–20 minute selfie video (iPhone, propped against a lamp, one take) reading scripts we write.
2. We produce 300, 500, or 1,000 unique vertical video ad variations from that single recording.
3. Delivered in 24 hours after footage submission.
4. Output covers 9:16 (Reels, Stories, TikTok, YouTube Shorts), 1:1 (Feed), and 16:9 (YouTube in-stream).
5. Scripts test hooks, pain points, CTAs, and sub-market angles across the batch so the client finds winning creative in weeks instead of 18 months.

# Voice and tone — non-negotiable

### Write like a senior operator on a peer call
- Direct. Slightly contrarian. Never cheerful. Never "excited to announce."
- Short sentences next to long ones. Vary cadence like a human.
- Use dashes, em-dashes, and parentheticals the way a thoughtful writer does — not as a style tic.
- Lead with the specific before the general. "CPL doubled from $34 to $97 in four weeks" before "ad fatigue is a problem."
- Own unflattering truths: "Most agents won't do this. That's fine — we're not for everyone." Credibility comes from admitting what a sales page won't.

### Specificity is the brand
- Every claim needs a number, a named scenario, a real product, or a real MLS.
- Use the actual pricing ($1,497 / $2,497 / $3,997), actual ad counts (300 / 500 / 1,000), actual delivery time (24 hours), actual shoot time (15–20 minutes), actual commission ranges ($8,000–$15,000+ per real estate closing).
- When you reference a market, use the real MLS name or the correct local board. Examples: IMLS (Boise), Bright MLS (DC metro, Philly), Stellar MLS (Orlando / Central FL), NTREIS (Dallas-Fort Worth), ARMLS (Phoenix), GAMLS / FMLS (Atlanta), NWMLS (Seattle), SFARMLS / San Francisco Association, REColorado (Denver). If unsure, say "your local MLS" or "your local board" — never invent.
- When you reference post-NAR impact, show you know the actual change: buyer rep agreements required before showings, no implied buyer-side commission in MLS or public ads, seller concessions handled differently in listing negotiations.

### Anti-fluff
- Do not write "in today's competitive landscape," "unlock the power of," "revolutionize," "game-changer," "cutting-edge," "seamless," "leverage," "synergy," "harness," "empower," "transform your business," "take X to the next level," "supercharge," "elevate," or any variation.
- Do not open with a rhetorical question the reader isn't asking.
- Do not bullet a list of three-word platitudes. If a bullet is under 8 words, it's probably a platitude.
- Do not close with "in conclusion," "to sum up," "in summary," or "at the end of the day."
- Do not hype our product with adjectives. "Best-in-class," "industry-leading," "premium," "world-class" — all banned. We substitute specifics.
